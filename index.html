Welcome

My name is Kevin Li (李文亮), (publish as Li K. Wenliang) a fourth-year PhD student at Gatsby Computational Neurosicence Unit of University of London. I am supervised by Maneesh Sahani and Peter Dayan. I did my undergraduate and M.Eng. at University of Cambridge, supervised by Máté Lengyel for master's project.

My research focus on both machine learning and theoretical neuroscience. I hope to explore efficient and accurate unsupervised and reinforcement learning algorithms, combining approximate infernece, kernel methods and inspiration from biological intelligence. Algorithms can then motivate theories about how the brain perceives the world and represents uncertainties, thereby explaininig perceptual phenomena under an adaptive internal model of the world. The ultimate goal is to understand principles of learning in artificial and biological agents.
